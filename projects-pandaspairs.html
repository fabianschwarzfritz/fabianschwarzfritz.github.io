Panda's Pairs

I've always wanted to build a simple game. With Panda's Pairs I designed, created and programmed a simple pair or memory game.

The game uses emoji's as content and is a single player game for players. I myself like to play this on my train rides, when I am waiting for an appointment or after work to rest a bit.

As I wanted to learn about how to integrate payments in an iOS app via Apple and AppStore's built in payment mechanism, I added additional card decks that users can purchase. Each card deck has a theme like "plants", "sports" or "cities". A lesson learned for me is how easy it is to define the products for sale in xcode but how much adjustment, follow-ups and configuration it needed to get the purchasable non-consumable through the appstore. I needed to configure prices, sales countries, add screenshots and many more. Also I forgot a few things as part of the review. One example is the "restore purchases" button that is required. Another example is to mark the app not as "made for kids" because it has access to purchasables
